# Summary

The `ETC Training` folder teaches Graydaze's Estimate to Complete workflow as the central project-finance control system. It combines definitions, process instructions, training decks, worked examples, and practice problems to show how PMs turn bids, change orders, QuickBooks data, and field expectations into a live projection of final job performance.

## Key Takeaways

- ETC is presented as both a projection of final project outcome and a management/accountability tool.
- The key mechanics are revised contract amount, revised cost budget, costs to date, variance, and percent complete.
- PMs are instructed to begin with QuickBooks costs to date, reconcile to ETC, and only include change-order revenue they are confident will be collected.
- The training examples connect ETC work to bidsheets, SOV changes, cost variance analysis, warchest planning, and forecasting tools.
- Practice materials emphasize developing unit costs and gross profit expectations for common painting and sealant scopes.

## Open Questions

- The 2024 deck says ETCs should be updated weekly, while the 2022 material says twice per month minimum. The newer cadence likely supersedes the older one but the schema should preserve both references.
