# Summary

The `PM 101 CRD` set is the core project-manager training manual plus companion lifecycle process maps and start-up checklists. It frames onboarding as a `Graydaze Power Launch`, introduces the `Graydaze Experience`, and translates those ideas into concrete pre-job, day-one, and ongoing job-ownership procedures.

## Key Takeaways

- The revised 2024 PM 101 manual appears to be the current canonical version, with a 2023 predecessor still present for reference.
- The training model combines culture, organizational health, safety onboarding, mentor-led shadowing, and field immersion rather than treating PM work as pure paperwork.
- The phase maps divide work into `Gear Up for Greatness` and `Own & Execute`, with later emphasis on communication, client closeness, and getting paid.
- The PM is expected to own contract review, scope understanding, finish schedules, SSSP preparation, dump meetings, initial ETC, vendor coordination, crew handoff, and day-one site leadership.
- Companion checklists operationalize startup work such as pre-mob planning, travel and housing requests, posted panel delivery, day-one field expectations, and scope-specific setup for vertical joints.
