# Thesis

The Graydaze training corpus is organized around one central outcome: creating project managers who can own a job end to end. The training program blends culture, technical field knowledge, travel logistics, finance systems, and repeatable checklists so that PMs are prepared to move from handoff to execution to billing without dropping operational control.

## Core Tracks

- PM training is the most detailed track and combines office onboarding, safety travel, finance and estimating immersion, PM shadowing, field training, PM 101 study, PTO, and testing.
- Estimating and sales tracks share some onboarding structure but branch into specialized systems and responsibilities.

## Repeating Operational Themes

- The PM owns the project after assignment.
- ETC is the core financial projection tool.
- Mission-support tasks such as billing, approvals, COs, and waivers are core PM work.
- Technical readiness is supported by glossaries, paint and sealant references, and scope-specific startup checklists.
- The culture layer is intentionally explicit: the `Graydaze Experience`, operating principles, and podcasts are part of how work is supposed to be performed.
- Training also includes lighter-weight operational memos and workshops on schedule building, startup detail, organization, and communication habits.
- Operational maturity is reinforced by templates, calculators, org references, and standard forms rather than by narrative manuals alone.

## Tensions

- The 2024 ETC material updates weekly while older 2022 material says twice monthly minimum.
- `PM 101 CRD Revised 10-23-24.pdf` likely supersedes the 2023 manual, but the exact chapter-level differences have not been mapped.
- Several assets are duplicated across folders, especially PM 101, Epicor, travel, and checklist materials.
- The raw training folder still contains some deferred or lower-signal items such as personalized checklists, autorecovered spreadsheets, quizlets, duplicate packaging, and low-extraction assets.
